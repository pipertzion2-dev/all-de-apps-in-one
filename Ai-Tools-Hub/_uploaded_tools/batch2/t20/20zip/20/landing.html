Why Engineers Estimate Hardware Costs Before Building

Hardware cost surprises kill projects. A component that looks affordable at prototype scale becomes a margin problem at 500 units. Estimating BOM costs before committing to a schematic lets you make smarter decisions — enclosure materials, MCU selection, connector specs — before expensive PCB spins and tooling orders.

01

Validate budget before you design

Know whether your concept fits the budget before you finalize the BOM and place component orders.

02

Communicate costs to stakeholders

Give investors, clients, or teammates a realistic cost picture at concept stage — not after production commits.

03

Identify expensive components early

Spot the line items that blow the BOM before designing around them. Swap materials, reduce complexity, or rescope.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hardware cost estimator?

A hardware cost estimator calculates the expected bill of materials (BOM) cost for a hardware product before production. It breaks down costs by component category — PCB, enclosure, connectors, controls, MCU — so engineers can validate budgets early in the design cycle without waiting for supplier quotes.

How accurate is the BOM cost estimate?

This estimator uses industry-standard component pricing to give directional accuracy typically within 10–25% of real supplier quotes. For production-validated, supplier-backed BOM pricing with version tracking, use the full Svivva hardware pipeline.

Is this estimator free? Do I need to sign up?

Completely free, no account required. Use it as many times as you want. To unlock the full production workflow — versioning, collaboration, supplier pricing, and production exports — use Svivva.

What hardware projects can I estimate?

Any hardware concept: custom PCBs, synthesizers, MIDI controllers, IoT sensors, wearables, industrial devices, consumer electronics prototypes, and short-run products. Works for solo makers, startups, and engineering teams.

Can I estimate costs for a custom PCB or enclosure?

Yes. The estimator covers standard 2-layer FR4 PCBs, birch ply and aluminum enclosures, connectors, user controls (potentiometers, switches, encoders), and MCU/USB interfaces. Describe your concept in plain language and get an itemized breakdown in seconds.

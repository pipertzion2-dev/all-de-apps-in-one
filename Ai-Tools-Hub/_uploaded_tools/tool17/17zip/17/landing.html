Why Engineering Teams Need a Unified Hardware Design Platform

Hardware development is broken across too many disconnected tools. Schematics live in one application, BOMs in a spreadsheet, mechanical files in a folder on someone's desktop, and version history in a chain of email attachments. The result is rework, missed cost overruns, and production delays that stem from information fragmentation — not engineering failure.

The Problem with Siloed Hardware Tools

Most hardware design software solves one part of the problem. EDA tools handle schematic capture and PCB layout but stop at the board boundary. BOM tools track components but don't connect to your schematic changes in real time. Project management software tracks tasks but can't read a Gerber file. When every step requires a different tool, the gaps between them accumulate into delays, errors, and cost overruns your team doesn't see coming.

Svivva was built to eliminate those gaps. The full hardware module connects concept, schematic, BOM, mechanical, and manufacturing prep into a single versioned pipeline. Changes at any stage propagate automatically — update a component in your schematic and your BOM cost estimate updates immediately. Revise your design and the previous version is preserved and reviewable. Your team works from one source of truth, not three competing spreadsheet versions.

Version Control Built for Hardware

Software engineering teams have had version control for decades. Hardware teams have historically relied on file naming conventions — "schematic_v3_FINAL_final2.sch" — and manual change logs that fall behind immediately. Svivva brings proper version control to hardware design: every revision is tracked, every change is attributable, and any previous state is restorable.

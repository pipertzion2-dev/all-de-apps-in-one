The fastest way to generate a hardware BOM online

Building a bill of materials is one of the most time-consuming parts of early-stage hardware development. Before a schematic is complete, engineers typically have no systematic way to estimate component count, package constraints, or procurement cost — which means critical feasibility decisions get made on guesswork.

The Svivva AI BOM generator changes that. By running a natural-language description through the BOM extraction stage of Svivva's hardware pipeline, you get a structured component list in seconds. This is not a template or a pre-filled example — it is the same extraction logic used inside the full Svivva platform, scoped to a single step so you can evaluate output quality before committing to a subscription.

Who uses an AI BOM generator?

Hardware engineers use it to scope a new design before opening their EDA tool. Product managers use it to get a rough component count and cost envelope for a roadmap conversation. Founders and makers use it to stress-test the feasibility of an idea before investing time in a full schematic. Procurement teams use it to get an early signal on lead times and part availability for a concept that hasn't reached the design phase yet.

Anywhere you need a rough BOM before the schematic exists, this tool gives you something to work with in under a minute.

How the Team Permission Simulator Works

The team permission simulator is a free online tool built by Svivva that generates a role-based permission map from a plain-language description of your team. Describe who is on your team, what you are building, and how large the collaboration is. The simulator analyzes your input and produces a table of suggested roles — such as Lead, Developer, QA, or Admin — along with their access levels for viewing, editing, and sharing project resources.

Who is this tool for?

This free team permission tool is designed for project managers, engineering leads, startup founders, and anyone setting up a new team or evaluating how permissions should be structured. It works across six categories: Hardware for electronics and physical product teams, Play for performance and creative workflows, Idea for early-stage concept scoring, Team for general collaboration and access control, Marketplace for API publishing readiness, and Build for digital-physical production flows. Each category tailors the output to the conventions and roles common in that domain.

What you get from the permission map

After clicking Generate, the simulator returns a permission map showing each role and whether it has View, Edit, or Share access. You also see a readiness score that reflects how much detail you provided — the more context you give about budget, materials, region, and team size, the higher the confidence in the generated map. This readiness indicator helps you gauge whether your team structure is well-defined enough to move into production tooling.

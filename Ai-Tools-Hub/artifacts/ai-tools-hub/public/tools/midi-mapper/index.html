MIDI Mapping for Hardware and Software

MIDI mapping is the process of assigning MIDI messages — notes, control change (CC) numbers, velocity, aftertouch — to specific parameters in your hardware synthesizer, drum machine, DAW, or plugin. A well-organized MIDI map lets you control filter cutoff, resonance, panning, volume, effects sends, and more from a single controller or sequencer. This tool generates that mapping table for you automatically based on your description, saving the manual work of building spreadsheets or patch sheets from scratch. Supported targets include kick, snare, hi-hat, bass, pads, lead, filter, resonance, pan, and custom parameters you define.

Audio Stem Export for Producers and Teams

Stem exporting is essential for mixing, mastering, collaboration, and live performance. Instead of bouncing a single stereo mix, stems let you separate your track into individual layers — drums, bass, synths, vocals — so each element can be processed, shared, or remixed independently. This preview tool shows you how Svivva handles that export step: your brief is interpreted, a stereo export is generated, and the result is paired with a MIDI mapping table that documents every routing decision.

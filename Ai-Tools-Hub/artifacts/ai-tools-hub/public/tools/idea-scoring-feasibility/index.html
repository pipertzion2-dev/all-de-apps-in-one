How to Score and Validate Your Idea Before You Build

Every successful product, hardware project, or creative venture starts with an idea. But not every idea is worth pursuing. The difference between a concept that gains traction and one that stalls often comes down to one thing: early validation. That is exactly what this free idea scoring and feasibility tool is designed to do — give you a fast, structured way to evaluate your concept before you commit real resources.

What is idea scoring?

Idea scoring is the process of evaluating a concept against a set of criteria to produce a readiness or feasibility score. Instead of relying on gut feeling, you define your idea in a short description, add optional constraints like budget, materials, or team size, and the tool calculates a score that reflects how well-defined and actionable your concept is. The more specific your input, the higher your readiness score — because specificity is a strong predictor of execution success.

Who is this tool for?

This idea scoring tool is built for makers, founders, product managers, engineers, and creative teams who need to evaluate concepts quickly. Whether you are designing a hardware product, planning a team collaboration workflow, scoping a digital build, or preparing something for a marketplace, the tool adapts to your category. It supports six modes — Hardware, Play, Idea, Team, Marketplace, and Build — each producing a different type of feasibility artifact tailored to that domain.

How the readiness score works

The readiness score is calculated based on the depth and detail of your concept description combined with the optional constraints you provide. A short, vague description produces a lower score. A detailed description with budget, materials, region, and collaboration size defined pushes the score higher. This mirrors real-world feasibility: the more clearly you can define what you want to build, who it is for, and what resources you have, the more likely you are to succeed. The score ranges from 35% to 95%, giving you a clear signal of where your idea stands.
